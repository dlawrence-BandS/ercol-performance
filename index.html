Analyst Commentary
Engagement & Soft Metrics

The add-to-basket rate has climbed from 0.41% during the onsite-only phase to 0.64% post-product expansion — a +56% improvement now above the pre-optimisation baseline of 0.57%. Measured year-on-year against the same spring window, the ATC rate has risen from 0.50% (Apr+May 2025) to 0.67% (Apr+May 2026) — a +34% like-for-like improvement, with total ATCs up +19.9%. Engagement rate has also improved consistently across all three phases (94.9% → 96.7% → 96.5%), confirming that the onsite content changes have made pages more relevant.

Sales & Revenue Performance

The full-year daily revenue average is not a useful benchmark — it is heavily distorted by November 2025 (Black Friday: £82,938) and January 2026 (£53,010). The year-on-year lens removes this skew. April 2026 was down vs April 2025, but product expansion only launched on 25 March — new pages had barely a week of indexing at April's start. By May 2026, revenue recovered to £23,363 — up +22.6% YoY. The contrast between the onsite-only window (7 purchases, £6,993 over 24 days) and the post-expansion period (70 purchases, £48,500 over 57 days) demonstrates that product catalogue depth is the primary conversion driver.

Ercol's Position vs Other Brands

Ercol is the #1 revenue-generating brand on the site — contributing over 27% of all brand-attributed revenue. It leads second-place Simba by 68% and third-place Pure Furniture by 88%. One important note: Simba recorded £149K in April 2026 alone — almost certainly a major promotion. Ercol's performance by contrast is structurally consistent, making it a more reliable long-term pillar. As new products accumulate organic rankings through Q3 2026, Ercol's revenue contribution is well-positioned to extend its lead.
